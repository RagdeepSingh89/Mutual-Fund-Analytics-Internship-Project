# Chart Insights

## 1. NAV Trend Analysis
Most mutual fund schemes showed a long-term upward NAV trend from 2022 to 2026. A strong bull run was visible during 2023, followed by a correction phase in 2024.

## 2. AUM Growth Analysis
SBI Mutual Fund maintained the highest AUM across all years and showed consistent growth from 2022 to 2025.

## 3. SIP Inflow Trend
Monthly SIP inflows steadily increased over time and reached an all-time high of approximately ₹31,002 Cr in Dec 2025.

## 4. Category Inflow Heatmap
Liquid Funds received the highest net inflows among all fund categories, indicating strong investor preference for liquidity.

## 5. Age Group Distribution
Investors aged 26–35 form the largest investor segment, accounting for approximately 41% of all investors.

## 6. Gender Distribution
Male investors represent around 66.5% of the investor base, while female participation is approximately 33.5%.

## 7. SIP Amount by Age Group
SIP investment amounts show a similar distribution across age groups, although several high-value outliers are present.

## 8. Geographic Distribution
Madhya Pradesh and Punjab contributed the highest total SIP amounts among the analyzed states. Tier-30 cities accounted for roughly two-thirds of investors.

## 9. Folio Count Growth
Mutual fund folio counts increased from 13.26 Cr in Jan 2022 to 26.12 Cr in Dec 2025, indicating strong growth in retail participation.

## 10. NAV Correlation Matrix
Most selected funds exhibited weak daily return correlations, suggesting diversification benefits.

## 11. Sector Allocation
Banking, IT, and Pharma sectors dominated portfolio allocations, accounting for a major share of overall holdings.
